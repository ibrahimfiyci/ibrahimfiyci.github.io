Technical Project

OSINT Threat Intelligence & AI Attack Surface Monitor

A defensive public-source intelligence workflow for tracking AI security signals, vendor advisories, exposed assets, and emerging risk themes.

Project Overview

The monitor collects open-source security signals, normalizes them into structured records, enriches them with severity and asset context, and generates concise markdown briefings for analysts reviewing defensive security work.

Technologies

  • Python
  • RSS and public advisories
  • CISA KEV-style enrichment
  • Markdown briefings
  • OSINT source tracking

Workflow

The project emphasizes lawful, defensive OSINT: public advisories, official bulletins, vendor updates, and open security research. It avoids intrusive scanning and focuses on turning public information into decisions that security teams can use.

Output

Each briefing explains what changed, why it matters, what assets or AI workflows could be affected, and which next steps a defender should consider.

Study notes

This project shows practical security communication, open-source intelligence discipline, and the ability to translate noisy public signals into structured cyber risk notes.
