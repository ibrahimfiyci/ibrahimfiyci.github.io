Securing Agentic AI Systems Before They Reach Production

By James Fiyci | Updated July 10, 2026

Agentic AI systems are powerful because they can act. That same capability makes security design more important than prompt wording alone.

Least privilege

Agents should receive the minimum data and tool access required for a task. Broad credentials, shared secrets, and unrestricted APIs make failures more damaging.

Approval gates

High-impact actions should require human review. Examples include sending external messages, modifying records, deleting data, or triggering financial and operational workflows.

Monitoring

Security teams need logs that explain intent, tool selection, data scope, policy decisions, and final outcome. Without that, incidents become hard to investigate.
