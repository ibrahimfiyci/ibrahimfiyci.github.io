2026 AI Security Watchlist

By James Fiyci | Updated July 10, 2026

AI security is moving from theory into daily enterprise operations. The most important areas to watch are agentic AI, AI bill of materials, prompt injection, model supply chain risk, and OSINT workflows that help teams track public signals.

Agentic AI security

Agentic AI systems can call tools, retrieve information, and take actions. This matters because these systems need practical controls: least privilege, approval gates, audit logs, and clear accountability.

AI SBOM and model inventory

CISA's AI SBOM guidance shows that AI systems need transparency around components, dependencies, models, datasets, and ownership. This creates a strong project area for security engineers who like supply-chain risk.

OWASP, MITRE ATLAS, and NIST AI RMF

OWASP helps organize LLM application risks, MITRE ATLAS gives a vocabulary for adversarial AI behavior, and NIST AI RMF helps teams reason about governance, mapping, measurement, and management.

OSINT for AI security

OSINT workflows can turn public advisories, vulnerability bulletins, vendor updates, and research into useful AI security briefings. The goal is not noise collection; it is clear risk communication.
